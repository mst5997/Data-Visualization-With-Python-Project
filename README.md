This project analyzes the U.S. Family Budget Dataset, which shows how the cost of living varies across counties and metro areas in the United States. It looks at different family types and breaks down major expenses like housing, food, transportation, healthcare, childcare, taxes, and income.

The goal is to explore the data using Python and visualizations to find patterns, compare states, and understand how costs change depending on location and family size. I clean the data, create charts and tables, and use different graphs to compare variables like income vs. taxes and childcare costs across states.

I also look for trends, correlations, and outliers, and use tools like regression, PCA, and 3D plots to dig deeper into the relationships between variables. Overall, the project helps show how the cost of living differs across the U.S. and what factors affect it the most.
